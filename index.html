About Me

I study how the brain uses prior experience to make sense of the world.

I am an NSERC-funded postdoctoral researcher and cognitive neuroscientist working with Dr. Keisuke Fukuda. My research examines how internally available information interacts with incoming sensory input, focusing on how memory and attention shape perception under uncertainty. Using behavioural methods, EEG, and computational modeling, I investigate how the brain integrates memory and sensory information to support perceptual inference in real time.

I previously completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Western University working with Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude. I earned my PhD in Psychology (Cognitive Neuroscience) at the University of Toronto in 2023, where I trained with Dr. Morris Moscovitch and Dr. Claude Alain, and completed my Honours BSc at McGill University working with Dr. Stephen McAdams.

My research program has expanded into a broader question: How does internally available information shape perception over time, across modalities, and across individuals?

At its core, my work seeks to understand how experience enables the brain to adapt to ambiguity, change, and the complexity of real-world environments.
