About Me

I study how the brain uses prior experience to make sense of the world.

My research examines how internally available information shapes perception over time, across modalities, and across individuals. Working at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience and experimental psychology, I combine behavioural methods, EEG, and statistical modeling to study how the brain integrates context and sensory information to support perceptual inference in real time.

I am an NSERC-funded postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Psychology at the University of Toronto, working with Dr. Keisuke Fukuda.

I previously completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Western University with Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude. I earned my PhD in Psychology (Cognitive Neuroscience) at the University of Toronto in 2023, where I trained with Dr. Morris Moscovitch and Dr. Claude Alain, and I completed my Honours BSc at McGill University in 2018, where I trained with Dr. Stephen McAdams.
